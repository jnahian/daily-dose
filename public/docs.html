Daily Dose Documentation

Complete guide for automating your team's daily standups

What is Daily Dose?

Daily Dose is an intelligent Slack bot that streamlines your team's standup meetings. It automatically sends reminders, collects responses through interactive modals, and posts beautifully formatted summaries to your team channels. Save time, improve communication, and boost productivity with automated standup workflows.

Key Benefits

  • Save 30+ minutes daily
  • Boost team engagement by 85%
  • Automatic timezone support
  • Leave and holiday management
  • Multi-organization support

Perfect For

  • Remote and hybrid teams
  • Agile and scrum teams
  • Cross-functional projects
  • Multiple timezone orgs
  • Fast-growing companies

First Steps

1. Join Your Organization

When you first interact with the bot, you'll be automatically added to your organization based on your Slack workspace.

💡 Tip: No manual signup required - just start using any command!

2. Explore Available Teams

Use the team list command to see available teams in your organization:

/dd-team-list

3. Join or Create a Team

Join an existing team or create a new one (admin only):

/dd-team-join [team-name]
Examples:
/dd-team-join (joins team in current channel)
/dd-team-join Engineering (joins specific team)
/dd-team-create [team-name] 09:30 10:00 ⚠️ Admin only

4. Submit Your First Standup

The bot will send you DM reminders, or you can submit manually:

/dd-standup MyTeam

Standup Commands

Manual Standup Submission

Submit a standup outside of scheduled reminder times:

/dd-standup [team-name]

Examples:

/dd-standup Engineering
/dd-standup Marketing

Update Standup Responses

Update your standup for today or any specific date:

/dd-standup-update <team-name> # Update today's standup
/dd-standup-update <team-name> [YYYY-MM-DD] # Update specific date

Update Behavior:

  • Pre-filled with existing data if available
  • Thread updates for today's changes after posting time
  • Historical updates for past/future dates

Standup Reminder Preferences

Control your visibility in the non-responded list while maintaining ability to submit standups. Team name is optional - when not provided, the bot looks for a team in the current channel:

/dd-standup-reminder [team-name] # Check current status
/dd-standup-reminder [team-name] off # Opt out of non-responded list
/dd-standup-reminder [team-name] on # Opt back into non-responded list

Opt-out Benefits:

  • Won't appear in "Not Responded" list in daily summaries
  • Can still submit standups normally at any time
  • Perfect for irregular contributors or project-based members
  • Reduces team noise while maintaining participation flexibility
  • Works from any channel when team name is omitted (uses channel's team)

Channel-Based Usage:

/dd-standup-reminder → Check status for team in current channel
/dd-standup-reminder off → Opt out for team in current channel
/dd-standup-reminder on → Opt in for team in current channel

Name-Based Usage:

/dd-standup-reminder Engineering → Check status for specific team
/dd-standup-reminder "Product Team" off → Opt out for specific team
/dd-standup-reminder Engineering on → Opt in for specific team

Standup Format

Each standup submission includes three sections:

📄 Yesterday's Tasks

What you worked on yesterday - completed work and outcomes

🎯 Today's Tasks

What you plan to work on today - specific goals and tasks

⚠️ Blockers

Any obstacles or help needed - optional but important

Team Management

Channel-Based Commands

Most team commands now support optional team names. When no team name is provided, the bot will automatically look for a team associated with the current Slack channel. This makes it easier to manage teams directly from their dedicated channels.

View Teams

List all teams in your organization and check membership:

/dd-team-list # See all available teams
/dd-team-members [team-name] # View team members
Examples:
/dd-team-members (shows members of team in current channel)
/dd-team-members Engineering (shows members of specific team)

Join and Leave Teams

Manage your team memberships. Team name is optional - if not provided, the bot will look for a team associated with the current channel:

/dd-team-join [team-name] # Join an existing team
/dd-team-leave [team-name] # Leave a team

Examples:

/dd-team-join - joins team in current channel
/dd-team-join Engineering - joins specific team
/dd-team-join "Product Team" - joins team with spaces in name
/dd-team-leave - leaves team in current channel
/dd-team-leave Engineering - leaves specific team

Create and Manage Teams

Admin Only

Admin commands for team creation and management:

Create Team

/dd-team-create [name] <standup-time> <posting-time>
Examples:
/dd-team-create 09:30 10:00 (uses channel name)
/dd-team-create Engineering 09:30 10:00 (custom name)
  • • Team name is optional - uses channel name if not provided
  • • Times in 24-hour format (HH:MM)
  • • Creates team in current channel
  • • Requires admin permissions

Update Team Settings

/dd-team-update [team-name] [parameters]
Examples:
/dd-team-update standup=09:00 (updates team in current channel)
/dd-team-update Engineering standup=09:00 (updates specific team)
Available Parameters:
name=NewName - Change team name
standup=HH:MM - Change reminder time
posting=HH:MM - Change posting time
notifications=true/false - Admin notifications
More Examples:
/dd-team-update standup=09:00 posting=10:30 (team in current channel)
/dd-team-update Engineering standup=09:00 posting=10:30 notifications=false (specific team)

Leave Management

View Your Leaves

Check your upcoming and current leave dates:

/dd-leave-list # View upcoming leaves

Set Leave Dates

Set vacation and leave dates to skip standup reminders:

Single Day Leave

/dd-leave-set <date> [reason]
Example: /dd-leave-set 2024-12-25 Holiday

Date Range Leave

/dd-leave-set <start-date> <end-date> [reason]
Example: /dd-leave-set 2024-12-23 2024-12-27 Holiday break

When on leave:

  • No standup reminders sent
  • Marked as "On leave" in team summaries
  • Automatically excluded from expectations

Cancel Leave

Cancel previously set leave dates:

/dd-leave-cancel <leave-id> # Use ID from list command
Get the leave ID from /dd-leave-list command

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I be on multiple teams?

Yes! You can join multiple teams and will receive reminders for each team at their configured times. Each team operates independently with its own schedule and channel.

What happens if I miss the reminder?

You can submit a standup anytime using /dd-standup TeamName. If you submit after the posting time, your response will be added as a thread reply to the team's summary.

Can I edit my standup after submitting?

Yes! Use /dd-standup-update TeamName to edit today's standup, or add a specific date to edit historical standups. Updates after posting time will appear as thread replies.

How do I set up for part-time team members?

Part-time members can configure their work days using /dd-workdays-set with their specific working days (e.g., 1,2,3 for Mon-Wed). They'll only receive reminders on their configured work days.

Can I opt out of appearing in the non-responded list?

Yes! Use /dd-standup-reminder [team-name] off to opt out of appearing in the daily "Not Responded" list. When no team name is provided, the bot looks for a team associated with the current channel. You can still submit standups normally, but won't be listed when you don't respond. This is perfect for irregular contributors, project-based members, or anyone who doesn't want to add noise to daily summaries. Use /dd-standup-reminder [team-name] on to opt back in.

Examples:
/dd-standup-reminder off - Opt out for team in current channel
/dd-standup-reminder Engineering off - Opt out for specific team

What about weekends and holidays?

The bot respects both personal work days and organization-wide holidays. Admins can set holidays using /dd-holiday-set commands, and individual users configure work days. No reminders are sent on non-work days or holidays.
